# Getting Comfortable
*A guide to navigating dorm life on campus*

![Virginia Tech dorm](vtdorm.jpg)

## Getting Into Your Dorm & Using Your Hokie Passport
You'll quickly learn that one of your most important tools on campus is your [Hokie Passport](https://www.hokiepassport.vt.edu/), known to seasoned VT students as your **Hokie P**.

You'll use your Hokie P to get into your residence hall and dorm room, buy food at the dining halls, check in at campus events, and even verify your identity during exams.

Most residence halls have several entrances, and these doors are locked to anyone who is not a resident of the respective hall. To gain entry, you'll have to scan your Hokie P on the card reader next to the door.

The same is true when you get to your dorm room. To unlock your door, you'll scan your Hokie P against the electronic lock under the door handle and input your room's entry code, which you received when you moved in.

    If you lose your Hokie P and get locked out of your room, don't be alarmed! On each floor of every residence hall, there are Student Leaders (SLs) who can help you. During your move-in orientation, the SLs also provide you with a "Duty Phone" number to call for any residential emergencies. 
 
## Roommate Agreement & StarRez Portal
Whether you picked your roommate or decided to try your luck with a "random," problems can arise in such close quarters. The roommate agreement will be a good tool for a smooth year in your dorm. This is your chance to hash out the big stuff early: room cleanliness, quiet hours, sharing personal items, and guest policies. 

A quick and honest conversation during your first week at Virginia Tech can save you from future debates about the trash piling up too high or whether 2 a.m. is an acceptable time for a Mario Kart tournament. 

To access your roommate agreement, log into your [StarRez portal](https://vt.starrezhousing.com/StarRezPortalX/145A51B9/1/1/Home-Home?UrlToken=7D47662B) and follow the submission instructions. 

## Laundry
Laundry is a rite of passage with campus living, and a true test of responsibility and timing. In the residence halls, you’ll be sharing laundry machines with your entire building, so make sure you don’t leave your clothes sitting in the dryers for hours. 

The laundry machines are pay-per-load through your Hokie Passport account, so keep an eye on your balance. To add funds to your Hokie Passport, login to the [Hokie Spa website](https://selfservice.banner.vt.edu/ssb/twbkwbis.P_GenMenu?name=bmenu.P_MainMnu), click on Hokie Wallet, then navigate to Hokie Passport Services. From there, you can add money to both your Hokie Passport and Dining Dollars balances.

If you'd like to pay for laundry from your phone, you can use [Laundry Web](https://housing.vt.edu/experience/Halls/services_amenities/laundry_rooms.html) or the Speed Queen app. Laundry Web allows you to reserve a washer or dryer, tells you how many machines are in use, and notifies you when your laundry is done.

If you don't feel like doing laundry yourself, Virginia Tech offers a laundry subscription service through their partnership with [University Cleaners](https://universitycleanersva.com/). With this service, your laundry will be washed, folded, and returned to your door within two days. 


## Future Housing
After your first year, it’s only natural to want some more space. Blacksburg has a ton of good options for off-campus housing after you've finished your freshman year. 

Every semester, Virginia Tech hosts an Off-Campus Housing Fair to display the plethora of housing options that are available to sophomores and upperclassmen. 

Keep an eye out on [GobblerConnect](https://gobblerconnect.vt.edu/) if you’re interested in attending!

If you loved living in a residence hall and want to return to campus in your sophomore year, there are options for you too. Students who want to return to the dorms must undergo an [application process](https://housing.vt.edu/contracts/apply/returning_undergrads.html) in late January for a chance to live on campus again.
