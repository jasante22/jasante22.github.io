Enjoy the Ambiance with Baan Thai

May 5,2021 by Joelle

After a long day of cranking out peer reviews for my Business Writing, it was about 8 pm. Perfect time to eat dinner. So escaping the quiet labs of the fourth floor of DDS, I went with two friends to a new restaurant that opened: Baan Thai.

Restaurant Information

Baan Thai is located near Kung Fu Tea on South Main Street, making it a semi-accessible location for students without cars. The restaurant has an out door seating area and everything is made fresh!

The Experience and What We Ordered

When we arrived, the restaurant had a good amount of people inside without feeling crowded. Between the three of us, we ordered Drunken Noodles, Tom Yum Soup, Pad Thai, and Pandan Coconut Custard. And then we waited.

One thing to really emphasize is that the restaurants makes their food fresh. As a result, the food does take a while to come out of the kitchen. This is not a restaurant to come alone to unless you can handle being in your own presence for at least 20 minutes and you have a book/phone to entertain yourself. Having friends for conversation makes the time easily fly.

All of the food was delicious. The tom yum soup is served in a small dish about the size of a miso soup bowl. The noodles came next, which were served on a leaf (likely a banana or pandan leaf?). Finally, the dessert! Since it had been a while, we got two custards, one on the house. The dessert was so good that I was really glad we had two to share among our small group of three.

Overall, highly recommend the restaurant! You may hear popping sounds of spring rolls freshly being prepared and the idle chatter of conversation. If you need a food break, take it at Baan Thai when you get the chance.
